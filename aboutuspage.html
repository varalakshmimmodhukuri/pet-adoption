Our Mission & Vision

Mission Statement

At PawsHaven, our mission is to rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome abandoned, abused, and neglected animals while educating the public about responsible pet ownership and animal welfare.

We strive to create a compassionate community where every animal receives the care, respect, and love they deserve, and where no healthy, adoptable animal is euthanized due to lack of space or resources.

Our Vision

We envision a world where all companion animals have loving homes, where animal cruelty is a thing of the past, and where the human-animal bond is celebrated and nurtured.

Through education, advocacy, and direct care, we work toward a future where shelters are no longer necessary because every pet has a permanent, loving home.

Our Story

From humble beginnings to becoming a leading animal welfare organization in the region, our journey has been driven by passion and purpose.

The Beginning

PawsHaven was founded in 2010 by Dr. Elizabeth Chen and James Wilson, two veterinarians who witnessed firsthand the overwhelming number of abandoned and neglected animals in the community. What began as a small rescue operation in Dr. Chen's garage quickly grew as more volunteers joined their cause.

With determination and community support, they secured their first facilityâ€”a modest converted warehouse that could house up to 30 animals. Despite limited resources, their dedication never wavered.

2010-2015: Building the Foundation

During our first five years, we established core programs including rescue operations, veterinary care, and adoption services. We formed partnerships with local veterinarians and businesses, hosted our first annual fundraising gala, and expanded our volunteer program to over 50 dedicated individuals.

2015-2020: Growth and Expansion

This period marked significant growth as we moved to our current facility, tripling our capacity. We launched our foster program, community education initiatives, and low-cost spay/neuter services. Our staff grew to include full-time animal behaviorists, allowing us to rehabilitate animals with special needs.

2020-Present: Innovation and Impact

In recent years, we've embraced technology to enhance our operations, implemented advanced medical treatments, and expanded our outreach programs. We've formed partnerships with national organizations, advocated for animal welfare legislation, and developed specialized programs for senior and special needs animals.
